Ukrainian borscht

Description

Known by a variety of names such as beet soup or red soup, Borscht is a traditional dish that has been enjoyed throughout Eastern Europe for centuries. The name "borscht" actually comes from the word "borshchov," which means "sour soup" in several languages.

While the sour flavor of can vary depending on the recipe, it is often achieved by adding sour cream and fresh dill weed to the soup. This iconic flavor combination is one of the hallmarks of this beloved dish, and a key part of what makes it so unique.

Ingredients

  • 2 to 2.5 lbs. bone-in beef (such as short ribs)
  • 10 cups water
  • 1 small onion, rough-chopped
  • 3 large bay leaves
  • 1 tsp. whole peppercorns

Steps

  1. Preheat large (preferably Dutch oven) pot on stove over medium-high heat. Drizzle with canola oil.
  2. Add short ribs to pot and sear for 2-3 minutes, until browned on all sides.
  3. Add water, onion, bay leaves, and peppercorn. Bring to a simmer and lower heat to low.
  4. Simmer for 2-4 hours, until meat is extremely fork tender and falling off the bone.
  5. Strain beef broth, discarding peppercorns and onions. Discard bones and set beef aside for later. Save the broth.
  6. In same pot, drizzle 2 Tbsp. canola oil over medium heat. Add onions and carrots and sauté until softened for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add smashed, minced garlic and cook for 1 minute more.
  8. Add beets, continue sautéing for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Add cabbage. Cook and sauté until softened.
  10. Over medium-low heat, add tomato paste, vinegar, sugar, salt, and black pepper, stirring until tomato paste is evenly dispersed, about 5 minutes.
  11. Break beef into small pieces and add to the pot.
  12. Stir in broth (enough to cover all veggies, but more if you like) and add potatoes.
  13. Cover and cook over medium-low heat for 30-35 minutes, or until potatoes are fork-tender.
  14. Stir in dill weed. Serve hot with a small spoonful of sour cream and sprinkle with extra dill.
